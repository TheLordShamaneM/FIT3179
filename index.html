Most Visited States

The data on tourist arrivals in Malaysia reveals that Johor is the most popular destination, attracting approximately 8.9 million visitors. In contrast, states like Labuan and Putrajaya experience much lower tourist numbers, with just 164,000 and 501,000 visitors, respectively.

Mode of Entry into Malaysia

Malaysia reveals that the predominant method is by land, with approximately 11.2 million tourists (about 56% of the total). This is followed by air travel, which accounts for around 7.4 million tourists (approximately 37%). Sea travel sees about 1.1 million tourists (around 6%), while rail is the least utilized mode, with only 388,781 tourists (about 2%).

Age Distribution in 2022 and 2023

The visitor data shows growth across all age groups from 2022 to 2023. The 15-24 group increased by 22.7%, from 31,838.44 to 39,065.91 visitors.

The 25-39 group saw the largest rise, growing by 26.5% from 54,598.35 to 69,075.64, remaining the most populous group.

Gender Based Analysis

The data shows the distribution of visitors by gender across different age groups. In the 15-24 age group, there were 26.71 million male visitors and 23.94 million female visitors.

The 25-39 age group had the highest number of visitors, with 43.85 million males and 38.87 million females.

In the 40-54 age group, there were 32.06 million male visitors and 29.96 million female visitors.

Ranked Local Tourism

In the local perspective, we see that Johor is not the most visited amongst the locals but instead it is Selangor. Selangor has been the highest-ranked visited state from 2017 to 2023.

The capital (Kuala Lumpur) is ranked 2nd by 2023, but it wasn't always as popular. In 2017, Perak was the second most visited. However, by 2023, it dropped to 4th.

In the end, we can see that almost all the states lost visitors during the time of COVID-19. As you can see from the line graph, there is a big dip in visitors between 2020 and 2022.
